Introduction to determinting density using graphical analysis¶

Density is the relationship between mass and volume, specificially $\frac{mass}{volume}$. The relationship equation is similar to a slope equation, $\frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x}$. Which means we can set the y-axis of the graph to mass and the x-axis to volume and generate a line whose slope is the density we want to find. However, most data don't line up in a perfect straight line. We can solve that with least-square analysis which generate a line of best fit that we can calculate a slope from.

The various slopes of various lines representing the relationships of mass versus volume is the density of the solid that it represents.

density of a New Penny is: $$5.8212\ g/ml$$

density of a Old Penny is: $$7.0279\ g/ml$$

Discussion¶

Pennies made before 1982 are mostly composited with copper (up to 95%) and the ones made after 1982 are made up of 95% Zinc. (“1909-1982 Pennies Worth More”) The relative density of Zinc is $7.134\ g/cm^{3}$ or $7.134\ g/ml$ (“Zinc - Element Information, Properties and Uses | Periodic Table”), which is about $2\ g/ml$ smaller compared to Copper, which has a density of $8.96\ g/cm^{3}$ or $8.96\ g/ml$.(“Copper    - Element Information, Properties and Uses | Periodic Table”) This means that newer coins would be significantly lighter compared to old ones.

We can use 2-Sample T-test to compare the density data of both types of coins and reference the p-value to see if there's a statical difference. Which should have a statical significant difference as the older coins are a lot heavier.

One way ANOVA Results:¶

Summary:

Name Sum Average Variance
New Penny 17.506 5.83533 0.0018
Old Penny 21.502 7.16733 0.00864

P-Value: 7.371663145136839e-06
F-Value: -29.9807762781856

The resulted p-value is significantly smaller than 0.05, which means there's no statical difference between new pennies's density and old pennies's densities.

Which means that our results refute the currently known composition of new and old coins as the tests shown no statical difference in between the data.
